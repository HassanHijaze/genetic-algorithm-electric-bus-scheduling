# Method Overview

This project is based on the master's thesis **"A Genetic Algorithm for Electric Bus Scheduling"**. The thesis addresses the Electric Bus Scheduling Problem, where a fixed set of service trips must be assigned to vehicle duties while satisfying both temporal and energy-feasibility constraints.

## Problem Setting

The input consists of:

- timetabled service trips,
- feasible deadhead movements,
- vehicle types and battery capacities,
- depot charging infrastructure,
- charging stations,
- grid-access limitations.

Every vehicle duty must begin and end at the depot. Since the vehicles are battery-electric, a duty must remain feasible with respect to the battery state of charge throughout the day. Energy is consumed by service trips and deadhead movements and is replenished during depot charging events.

The optimization objective combines:

- fleet size,
- total duty time,
- total driven distance.

The goal is therefore to cover all service trips exactly once while minimizing the total operating cost and respecting temporal, energy, and depot-charging constraints.
